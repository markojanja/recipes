Quattro Formaggio Pizza

Description

Quattro formaggi is a variety of Italian pizza topped with a combination of four kinds of cheese, as the name suggests. Traditionally, the cheeses should be mozzarella and three other, local cheeses, depending on the region, such as Gorgonzola, Fontina, and Parmigiano-Reggiano.

Optionally, the pizza can be further enriched with the addition of basil and tomato. The combination of cheeses gives the pizza an unusual, unique flavor that is loved by cheese-aficionados all over the world.

Ingredients:

  • Flour
  • Mozzarel
  • Fontina
  • Grana Padano
  • Gorgonzola
  • Tomato sauce
  • Basil
  • Cherry tommato

Steps:

  1. First, put the flour, salt and yeast in a bowl making sure to put the salt and yeast at opposite sides
  2. Add the water and olive oil and mix together to form a rough dough
  3. Shape the dough into a ball then knead on a lightly floured surface for around 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic
  4. After kneading shape the dough into a ball and place in a large clean bowl lightly greased with olive oil. Cover with plastic wrap and sit the dough in a dry warm place for 2-3 hours or until tripled in size
  5. Once the dough has risen, knock the air out by pressing down on it with your fist. Divide in half and shape into two separate balls ready for baking
  6. You can either shape the dough with your hands or roll it out. Place it on a tray sprinkled with semolina or flour
  7. Spread the base with tomato sauce then top with cheese
  8. Bake the pizza in a pre-heated oven until golden, bubbling and melted. Let it sit for 5 minutes before cutting
  9. Decorate with basil and cherry tomatto
